How Weather Conditions Influence Forest Fires

Forest fires are significantly influenced by various weather conditions. Understanding these factors is crucial for predicting and preventing wildfires.

1. Temperature

High temperatures dry out vegetation, making it more susceptible to ignition. Prolonged heatwaves can worsen the risk.

2. Humidity

Low humidity levels result in dry air, which dries out vegetation and increases fire risk. Relative humidity below 30% is considered hazardous.

3. Wind Speed

Strong winds can rapidly spread fires by carrying embers over long distances. Wind also provides oxygen, intensifying the flames.

4. Rainfall

While rainfall can prevent fires, lack of precipitation leads to drought conditions, increasing fire susceptibility.

Conclusion

Monitoring these weather patterns is essential for early fire detection and prevention. Tools like Fire Weather Indices (FWI) use weather data to predict fire risks.
