What Do These Inputs Mean?

  1. Temperature (째C): - Measures how hot it is.
    - Typical Range: 0째C to 40째C.
    - Higher temperatures dry out fuels, increasing fire risk.
  2. Relative Humidity (%): - Amount of moisture in the air.
    - Typical Range: 10% to 100%.
    - Lower humidity dries vegetation faster, increasing flammability.
  3. Wind Speed (km/h): - Measures wind that can spread fire.
    - Typical Range: 0 to 50+ km/h.
    - Higher speeds help fires spread more rapidly.
  4. Rainfall (mm): - Amount of recent precipitation.
    - Typical Range: 0 to 10+ mm (past 24 hours).
    - More rain generally reduces fire risk by moistening fuels.
  5. FFMC (Fine Fuel Moisture Code): - Indicates moisture content in surface litter and small branches.
    - Range: 0 to 101.
    - High FFMC (above 85) means drier fuels and higher flammability.
  6. DMC (Duff Moisture Code): - Measures moisture in loosely compacted organic layers (duff).
    - Range: 0 to 150+.
    - Higher DMC means deeper forest fuels are dry and burnable.
  7. ISI (Initial Spread Index): - Estimates how fast a fire will spread immediately after ignition.
    - Range: 0 to 50+.
    - Influenced by wind and FFMC; higher values = faster spread.
